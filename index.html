PREDATOR

Predator!!!

A "predator" is an animal that hunts and kills other animals for food. The term can also refer to a person or entity that exploits or takes advantage of others, often in a harmful or unethical way. For example, a "financial predator" might exploit vulnerable individuals for monetary gain.

HYANE

A hyena is a carnivorous mammal known for its scavenging habits and distinctive laugh-like vocalizations. Hyenas are often found in Africa and are known for their strong jaws and pack behavior. They play a crucial role in the ecosystem by cleaning up carcasses.

CROCODIALE

A crocodile is a large, carnivorous reptile that lives in tropical regions, primarily near water sources like rivers, lakes, and swamps. Crocodiles are known for their powerful jaws, sharp teeth, and armored bodies. They are skilled hunters, often ambushing their prey by lurking just below the water's surface before striking with speed and force. Crocodiles are among the oldest living reptiles, with a lineage dating back millions of years.

TIGER

A tiger is a large, powerful carnivorous mammal known for its distinctive orange coat with black stripes. Tigers are native to Asia and are the largest members of the cat family, known as Panthera tigris. They are solitary animals and are apex predators, meaning they are at the top of the food chain with no natural predators.

VULTURE

A vulture is a large bird of prey known for its scavenging habits. Vultures primarily feed on the carcasses of dead animals, playing a crucial role in the ecosystem by helping to clean up and recycle nutrients. They have strong beaks for tearing flesh and excellent eyesight to spot food from high altitudes. Vultures are often associated with death due to their diet, but they are vital in preventing the spread of disease by consuming decaying animal remains.
